Reimagining Agility: Shaping the Future of Agile – Brussels Workshop

Shaping the Future of Agile

📅 October 7, 2026 📍 Brussels, Belgium ⏱ 4 hours (afternoon) 👥 Max 50 participants
Register Your Interest

Something Has Stalled.
Let's Talk About It.

Agile practices are widespread. Certifications abound. Yet many organizations are experiencing more control with less learning, and growing fatigue rather than adaptability.

The Agile conversation has stalled. It's time to pause, ask uncomfortable questions, and reimagine what agility needs to become in a world shaped by scale, governance, and AI.

What This Workshop Is About

🚫

Not a conference

🚫

Not a panel discussion

🚫

Not a framework introduction

This is a space for practitioners, leaders, and teams to sit with complexity and learn in public. Over four hours, we'll work in small, self-directed groups to explore the "wicked problems" that don't have clear owners or clean solutions—the tensions many of us are already living with but rarely get time to explore together.

What We'll Explore

Eight wicked problems at the heart of Agile today. No prescribed answers—just honest conversation and shared learning.

01  Creating Agile Leadership & Culture

Are organizations still run as machines while expecting people to behave as part of a values-based system? We'll explore how leaders at all levels can shift from designing work to developing people's capability—creating spaces to learn how to deal with uncertainty and being explicitly judged on becoming wiser tomorrow than today.

02  Fostering Innovation & Continuous Learning

Organizations celebrate speed and delivery while crowding out learning and innovation. We'll surface what blocks progress and point to entrepreneurial mindsets, lightweight frameworks, and the explicit balancing of discovery, delivery, and product as ways to keep innovation alive.

03  Scaling Agile Across Organizations

Scaling without first addressing mindset, culture, and context simply reproduces hierarchy while calling it Agile. We'll challenge one-size-fits-all approaches and shift toward shared principles, coherent steering, and first steps that create satisfaction and vision.

04  The Future of Frameworks

Guidelines over frameworks. Pragmatism over dogma. Co-creation over enforcement. Evolution over stagnation. Frameworks are a starting frame, not a fixed future—useful only insofar as they enable learning and adaptation.

05  Aligning Agile with Business Value & Governance

Governance is not the enemy of agility. The real problem is confusion: governance, compliance, risk management, and legislation are repeatedly conflated. We'll focus on making decision-making, rules, measures, and value explicit.

06  AI and Automation

The next 1–3 years will be a shift toward human + AI collaboration, not automation replacing people. We'll explore trust, safety, data management, security, and ethics as non-negotiables—and why individuals and interactions become even more critical as processes speed up.

07  Bridging Agile and Traditional Project Management

The tension isn't Agile versus project management—it's a disconnect between strategy and behaviour. Without alignment on intent and how people act, neither Agile practices nor project controls can close the gap.

08  The Impact of AI on Leadership

Does AI change how leaders work, or does it fundamentally change what leadership is? As AI takes over coordination, analysis, and execution, what remains human work? Sense-making, ethical judgment, cultivating human connection, setting boundaries, shaping shared context.

About This Initiative

This workshop is part of the global Reimagining Agility initiative by Agile Alliance—a movement to refresh, extend, and make Agility accessible to all. We're re-examining core values and principles, extending them into new communities (product management, non-IT organizational units, enterprise agility), and creating spaces for intentional listening beyond the current climate.

Reimagining Agility isn't about finding answers or even agreeing. It's about creating space to surface tensions that many people are already living with but rarely get time to explore together.
